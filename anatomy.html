Spinal Anatomy Overview

The human spine is a complex structure consisting of bones, discs, ligaments, muscles, and nerves working together to provide support, flexibility, and protect the spinal cord.

Vertebral Regions

  • Cervical (7 vertebrae): Supports the head and allows for neck movement.
  • Thoracic (12 vertebrae): Anchors the ribs and supports the upper back.
  • Lumbar (5 vertebrae): Bears the weight of the lower back.
  • Sacral (5 fused vertebrae): Forms the back of the pelvis.
  • Coccygeal (4 fused vertebrae): The tailbone, a remnant of evolutionary ancestry.

Intervertebral Discs

Between vertebrae lie shock-absorbing discs composed of a gelatinous nucleus pulposus and tough annulus fibrosus. These discs allow flexibility and cushioning.

Ligaments and Muscles

Several strong ligaments stabilize the spine, including the anterior longitudinal ligament and ligamentum flavum. Surrounding muscles control movement and posture.

Spinal Cord and Nerves

The spinal cord runs through the vertebral canal and transmits signals between the brain and body. Nerve roots exit through foramina, innervating limbs and organs.
