Spine Anatomy Overview

The human spine, or vertebral column, is a central structure that provides support, mobility, and protection to the spinal cord. It consists of 33 vertebrae divided into five regions:

  • Cervical (7): Supports the head and allows for a wide range of motion.
  • Thoracic (12): Anchors the ribs and supports the upper back.
  • Lumbar (5): Bears the weight of the upper body; common area for lower back pain.
  • Sacral (5, fused): Connects the spine to the pelvis.
  • Coccygeal (4, fused): The tailbone.

Vertebrae Structure

Each vertebra consists of a vertebral bodyThe anterior portion of the vertebra that bears weight., a vertebral arch, and several processes for muscle and ligament attachment. Between most vertebrae are intervertebral discsShock-absorbing fibrocartilage cushions..

Spinal Cord and Nerves

The spinal cord runs through the vertebral foramen and is protected by the bony structure. It connects to peripheral nerves that branch out to various parts of the body, playing a critical role in motor control and sensation.

Facet Joints and Ligaments

Facet joints allow for controlled movement, while ligaments like the anterior longitudinal ligament, posterior longitudinal ligament, and ligamentum flavum help maintain alignment and stability.

Conclusion

Understanding the complex and elegant structure of the spine is essential for diagnosing spinal disorders, planning treatments, and appreciating the body's biomechanics. Stay curious and keep learning!
