Resources for Spine Education

Explore a curated selection of trusted and high-quality resources about spinal anatomy, physiology, pathology, and care. Ideal for medical students, health professionals, and curious learners.

Textbooks

  • Gray's Anatomy – Comprehensive anatomical reference with detailed spine illustrations.
  • Essentials of Spinal Disorders – Focused on pathology, diagnosis, and management.
  • Clinically Oriented Anatomy by Moore – Excellent for practical spinal clinical correlation.

Websites

  • Spine-Health.com – Patient-friendly educational content with videos and expert articles.
  • AANS Spine Section – Official neurosurgical insights on spine disorders.
  • PubMed Central – Access full-text scholarly articles about spine research.

Videos & Visual Aids

  • AnatomyZone – 3D spinal anatomy tutorials on YouTube.
  • Khan Academy Medicine – Spine and neurology education for all levels.

Apps

  • Complete Anatomy – Interactive 3D spine models for iOS/Android.
  • Essential Anatomy 5 – Excellent for studying spinal nerves and vertebrae.

Podcasts

  • The Back Doctor Podcast – Insights from spine specialists and patient journeys.
  • AO Spine Podcast – For professionals interested in spine surgery and research.

Research Journals

  • Spine (Wolters Kluwer) – Leading journal on spinal disorders and interventions.
  • European Spine Journal – Covers biomechanics, rehabilitation, and surgery.
