Common Spinal Pathologies

The spine can be affected by various degenerative, traumatic, congenital, and inflammatory conditions. Understanding these pathologies is key for prevention and treatment.

Degenerative Disc Disease

Age-related wear causes discs to lose hydration and elasticity, resulting in pain, reduced flexibility, and possible nerve compression.

Herniated Disc

A tear in the annulus fibrosus allows the nucleus pulposus to protrude, irritating nearby nerves and causing pain or numbness.

Scoliosis

An abnormal lateral curvature of the spine, often diagnosed in adolescence, potentially leading to pain and functional issues.

Spinal Stenosis

Narrowing of the spinal canal compresses nerves, causing pain, weakness, and sensory changes.

Osteoporosis

Loss of bone density increases the risk of vertebral fractures, which can cause deformity and chronic pain.

Trauma

Fractures, dislocations, and spinal cord injuries from accidents require immediate medical attention and can cause permanent disability.
